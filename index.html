Polar Bears are in Danger

Polar bears are in grave danger of extinction as a result of climate change.

Scarcity of food is growing

As sea ice disappears for longer and longer periods during the late summer, polar bears are left with insufficient time to hunt.

Climate Warming

Without significant reductions in carbon emissions and climate stabilization, we may lose all but a few polar bear populations by the end of the century.


Population Decrease

In southern portions of their range around Hudson Bay, Canada, there is no sea ice during the summer, and the polar bears must live on land until the bay freezes in the fall, when they can again hunt on the ice. While on land during the summer, these bears eat little or nothing. The bears have fewer cubs.

Food Scarcity

Polar bears can only exist in locations where the waters freeze, which enables them to hunt seals that live beneath, on, or in the frozen polar ice cap. Exacerbating the loss of hunting areas, it is projected that the melting polar ice cap would also result in a drop in the polar bears' preferred prey—seals.

Climate Warming

Climate change necessitates a paradigm shift in conservation—we can no longer put a fence around the polar bear's melting habitat or post wildlife cops to watch the entrance. The only way to restore the climate to its natural state is to completely phase out fossil fuels as an energy source.
